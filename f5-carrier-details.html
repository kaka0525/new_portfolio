F5 Networks Carrier Web Front-End

An intuitive web-based platform that manages and visualizes testing efforts

Overview

As F5 Networks strove to improve its product quality standards, it pushed the organization to optimize test coverage. A lot of relatively unknown testing frameworks that had a successful track record started to attract more attention. Often times, the soon-to-be expanded test environments were headless; they lacked a robust, impactful front-end that was ready for a wider audience who were not familiar with the framework to interact with and gain immediately insights from.

Carrier was the latest test framework that was in need to establish a broad frontend capability to meet new business needs. I was tasked to design and build the Carrier Graphical User Interface within the existing backend in an effort to help expand its test scope.

Client

My Role

I led the UX/UI design and front-end development of the on-page, web-based test case management tool. I worked alongside 2 test engineers and a Product Manager throughout the project. I stopped working on the project during the final back-end integration phase.

The Challenge

Our goal for the project was to build a test management tool to onboard more engineers and project managers to Carrier, the soon-to-expand scalable testing framework. Our ambition was to develop new process, design and standards, which other vertical teams can follow when they are expanding their infrastructure tools.

Our high level goals were to:

  1. Simplify test information into more understandable pieces that allows users to gain quick insights about test suites that have run.
  2. Build a collaborative system that allows other teams to integrate their test cases in Carrier.
  3. Create a platform that enables Carrier to continuously expand its reach and test scopes.

Research

To understand the problem domain, project scope, and opportunities at a high level, I conducted interviews with stakeholders to comprehend the wider business priorities, pain points and long-term strategies.

I shadowed Carrier test engineers when they were performing their dailytasks to get a better grasp of the existing process. And from the interviews and shadowing, I documented what are some areas of improvement and observed where do the pain points and frustrations lie.

From the user research, I discovered the following:

  1. Carrier testing environment is a black box in the eyes of the people who are not within the immediate team. It relies heavily on the tribal knowledge of the two Carrier test engineers to interpret its test results to the extent that even a simple pass and fail requires manpower to decipher.

Planning & Making

From the user research, I constructed personas and explored their challenges, motivations and goals to determine the use cases and flows that captures the right value proposition of each personas. I then partnered up with a Project Manager on prioritizing feature requests, personasâ€™ needs, business objectives and tech feasibility against each other and created a list of what features needs to focus on supporting for the launch version of the product

Ideation

With data representation being the backbone of the product, I planned and prioritized strategically on what contextual data are critical to show users on the 1st phase of the project and investigated how information flows across the organization.

By doing so, it allowed me to focus for exploring different solutions on representing data in an easy to follow and engaging way, and decided what additional data might be useful for later updates.

I presented sketches in weekly sync ups to stakeholders to assure the design reflects the changes that we discussed and actively obtained regular feedback and input to expose gaps in requirements and flow. Once we agreed on the product features solution, I translated the concepts into fully functional interactive prototype in HTML, CSS and JavaScript. Usability testing is done throughout the whole project cycle to validate the concept and I iterated rapidly to discover the optimal user experience to ensure the information architecture of the web portal is effective.

Solution

The purpose of the tool is to help the test framework expand by ensuring the design presents information in an effective and intuitive format that makes sense to the audience. Hence, empowering teams to work more productively and efficiently. The design principle is to inform with a glance without the need of additional training. I used familiar design patterns so users donâ€™t need to analyze each interaction to navigate through the tool.

Real time insights of the test environment

A key design challenge was to consider how to illustrate continuous testing data. I opted for the gantt chart inspired design to lists all the test cases that are scheduled and performed on the vertical axis, and time intervals on the horizontal axis. The width of the horizontal bars in the graph shows the duration of each activity. The dynamic timeline depicts the status/state of individual tests with a glance, so users can easily get real-time insights on the testing process and easily track statuses of individual tests.

Simple & Digestible Test Results

The timeline combines high performance and ease of use to let users who are not familiar with the Carrier framework be informed on test results in an easily understandable format. Users can zoom-in-to reveal the results broken down by the test branch and build in easy to comprehend terms. And hover over the individual tests to see what tests fail and links to the logs. Hence, this brings users a high level understanding of the characteristics and trends of the the test framework.

Efficient Proposal Form

Test Requesters will be asked to fill in a test proposal form, which captures the essential information that are needed to set up the new Carrier test configuration. The form is designed to be simple to follow so they can quickly go through them without confusion. And with the data, Carrier engineers can plan and make adjustments to the test framework accordingly to ensure the proposed test can merge in with the existing tests harmoniously.

Tracks Tasks To Meet Deadlines

Carrier Task boards is designed to provide a central place for users to organize and manage all of the new test proposals progress in a structured way.

The new test management page helps project managers to stay up-to-date on all test proposals. For test requesters, they can use the tool to set deadlines and priorities to ensure the new test automation will get done on time. And Carrier Engineers can communicate and delegate the subtasks to the test requester if there is any missing information they need to set up for the new automation. In addition, the tool helps them plan their time and meet deadlines without the need to search through emails and notes for to-dos.

Test Management In A Structured Way

The task board supports the classic CRUD operations, which permit users and to create, read, update and delete test proposals statuses accordingly. Users can assigning the next to-do items to owners and enable collaboration with comments. And e-mail notifications will be sent to the next-action owner upon status change.
