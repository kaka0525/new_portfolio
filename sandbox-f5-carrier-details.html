SANDBOX F5 Networks Tool

An intuitive web-based platform that manages and visualizes testing efforts

Overview

As F5 Networks strives to improve its product quality standards, it pushes the organization to optimize test coverage. A lot of relatively unknown testing frameworks that have had a successful track record started to attract more attention. Often times, the soon-to-be expand test environment are headless; they lack a robust, impactful front-end that is ready for a wider audience who are not familiar with the framework to interact with and gain immediately insights from.

Carrier, is the up-and-coming testing framework/latest test framework that is in need to establish a broad frontend capability to meet new business needs. I was tasked to design and build the Carrier Graphical User Interface within the existing backend in an effort to help expand its test scope.

Client

My Role

I led the UX/UI design and front-end development of the on-page, web-based test case management tool. I worked alongside 2 test engineers and Product Manager throughout the project. I stopped working on the project during the final back-end integration phase.

The Challenge

Our goal for the project is to build a test management tool to onboard a wider audience to Carrier, the soon-to-expand scalable testing framework. Our ambition is to develop new process, design and standards, which other vertical teams can follow when they are expanding their infrastructure tools.

Our high level goals were to:

  1. Simplify test information into more understandable pieces that allows users to gain quick insights and overview on the test framework
  2. Build an effective communication system that allows other teams to integrating and run their test cases in Carrier.
  3. Create a platform that enables Carrier to continuously expand its reach and test scopes.

Research

To understand the problem domain, project scope, and opportunities at a high level, I conducted interviews with stakeholders to comprehend the wider business priorities, pain points and long-term strategies.

I shadowed Carrier test engineers when they were performing their daily tasks to get a better grasp of the existing process. And from the interviews and shadowing, I documented what are some areas of improvement and observed where do the pain points and frustrations lie.

From the user research, I discovered the following:

  1. Carrier testing environment is a black box in the eyes of the people who are not within the immediate team. It relies heavily on the tribal knowledge of the 2 Carrier test engineers to interpret its test results to the extent that even a simple pass and fail requires manpower to decipher.
  2. The ability to quickly identify the real-time status and test configuration (test build and branch) of the environment is deemed highly desirable.
  3. Ability to find Carrier’s latest status on test scope expansion plan is noted to be an important factor to keep all teams align.
  4. The test expansion process is anxiety inducing for Carrier engineers. They worried the incoming new tests proposals from other teams do not have enough of the understanding in the environment to provide the necessary information (conditions or configurations) that is needed to set up for the new automation or worst affecting the results of the existing tests.

Planning & Making

From the user research, I constructed personas and explored their challenges, motivations and goals to determine the use cases and flows that captures the right value proposition of each personas. I then partnered up with project manager on prioritizing feature requests, personas’ needs, business objectives and tech feasibility against each other and created a list of what features needs to focus on supporting for the launch version of the product

Ideation

With data representation being the backbone of the product, I planned and prioritized strategically on what contextual data are critical to show users on the 1st phase of the project and investigate on how information flows across organization.

By doing so, it allows me to focus on exploring different solutions on representing data in an easy to follow and engaging way. And decided what addition data might be useful for later updates.

I presented sketches in weekly sync ups to stakeholders to assure the design reflect the changes that we discussed and actively obtain regular feedback and input to expose gaps in requirements and flow. Once we agreed on the product features solution, I translated the concepts into fully functional interactive prototype in HTML, CSS and JavaScript. Testing is done throughout the whole project cycle to validate the concept and I iterated rapidly to discover the optimal user experience to ensure the information architecture of the web portal is effective.

The Product

The purpose of the tool is to to help the test framework expand by ensuring the design present information in an effective and intuitive format that makes sense to the audience. Hence, empowering teams to work more productively and efficiently. The design principle is to inform with a glance without the need of additional training. I used familiar design patterns so users don’t need to analyze each interaction to navigate through the tool.

Solution

Real time insights of the test environment

A key design challenge was to consider how to illustrate continuous testing data. I opted for the gantt chart inspired design to lists all the test cases that are scheduled and performed on the vertical axis, and time intervals on the horizontal axis. The width of the horizontal bars in the graph shows the duration of each activity. The dynamic timeline depicts the status/state of individual tests with a glance, which users can easily get real-time insights on the testing process and easily track statuses of individual tests.

Simple & digestible test results

The timeline combines high performance and ease of use to let users who are not familiar with the Carrier framework be informed on test results in an easily understandable format.Users can zoon-in-to reveal the results broken down by the test branch and build in easy to comprehend terms. And hover over the individual tests to see what tests fail and links to the logs. Hence, bring users a high level understanding of the characteristics and trends of the the test framework.

Efficient Proposal Form

Test Requesters will be asked to fill in a test proposal form, which captures the essential information that are needed to set up the new Carrier test configuration. The form is designed to be simple to follow so they can quickly go through them without confusion. And with the data, Carrier engineers can plan and make adjustments to the test framework accordingly to ensure the proposed test can merge in with the existing tests harmoniously.

Track Tasks to meet deadlines

It is natural that an existing mentorship enter the maintenance phase. Another way to promote users to notify the program when the mentorship is inactive is to provide incentives. Since we have a limited mentors, all mentees can only be paired with one mentor. And it is common that the challenges the mentees face when they are starting out are different from what they face 2 to 3 years in the industry. Later in the career, mentees might want to seek out different mentors to expand their network or get a fresh perspective and guidance.

If a mentee already been assigned to a mentor and wants to initiate new match, we provide a pop up that is shown explaining the new pairing request is prohibited. There is information that inform them that all mentees can only be assigned to one mentor. In order for them to request a different mentor, they have to pause on the current pairing before they can proceed with the new mentor request. The popup helps users to be accustomed to the idea of “pausing a pairing” and minimize user’s frustration. And mentees are encouraged to maintain connections with previous mentors who they can turn to when desired.

Progressive Personal Information Disclosure

The PuPPy matching process will focus on self selection. It raised the question on how much information to display when mentee is browsing through mentor’s profiles. The application takes privacy very seriously . Before the match, only mentor’s usernames, expertise, introduction are displayed. It does not release the mentor’s full name, slack handler, e-mail information until mentors accept the match request.

Iteration

After a few rounds of iteration and online usability tests on the low-fi mockhelps that help identify shortcomings and pain points, I helped the team to narrow down and created consensus on what the product and user flow will be like. And continuous testing was done throughout the project and prototypes were shared with stakeholders to obtain feedback. Once the team had confidence on the overall design, I began to digitalize the design

Design

Intended to be modern and distraction-free, the style of design places emphasis on important information such as call-to-actions, and contact information.

The Product

By the end of phase one of the Hackathon, we completed the core functionality of the product and delivered a presentation to share the prototype with the community.

During the application presentation, we highlighted the major features and presented the vision we have for the application. The project owner was happy with the design and reception of the project is positive. The demo also served the purpose to persuade developers to support its vision and cause.

It was incredibly meaningful and rewarding to examine the mentorship experience and create an open source solution that helps the tech community to implement their mentorship program.
